1. Introduction

The rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) has opened new possibilities for automated data analysis and insight generation. However, when applied to tabular data analysis, LLMs face significant challenges related to faithfulness, numeric reasoning, grounding, and evaluation. Current systems often generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect insights, suffer from hallucinations about trends and statistical patterns, and lack proper provenance linking claims to underlying data.

This research addresses the critical gap between LLM capabilities and the requirements for trustworthy, explainable insight generation from tabular data. In domains such as business intelligence, healthcare analytics, financial reporting, and scientific data analysis, automated insight generation must be both accurate and explainable.

Research Focus: This research proposes a comprehensive framework that combines deterministic statistical computation with LLM-based narrative generation, incorporates explicit grounding and provenance mechanisms, develops novel evaluation methods that prioritize numeric faithfulness, and conducts rigorous user studies to assess trust and utility in real-world analytical workflows.

2. Problem Statement

Core Challenge: Despite the proliferation of LLM-based data analysis tools, there exists a fundamental disconnect between the narrative fluency of generated insights and their factual correctness, particularly when analyzing structured tabular data. This discrepancy undermines trust and limits practical adoption in professional settings where accuracy is paramount.

2.1 Faithfulness & Numeric Reasoning Deficiencies

Large Language Models exhibit persistent weaknesses in numerical computation and statistical reasoning. When analyzing tabular data, they frequently:

  • Hallucinate trends, patterns, and relationships not supported by the underlying data
  • Make arithmetic errors in basic calculations (percentages, averages, differences)
  • Misinterpret statistical significance and correlation versus causation
  • Generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect numeric assertions
  • Struggle with time-series analysis, seasonal decomposition, and trend extrapolation

These deficiencies stem from the training objective of next-token prediction rather than veridical reasoning, making LLMs unreliable for numeric analysis without verification mechanisms.

2.2 Lack of Grounding and Provenance

Current insight generation systems fail to provide transparent connections between generated narratives and source data:

  • No explicit indication of which specific data cells support each claim
  • Missing lineage tracking from statistical computation to narrative formulation
  • Inability to trace insights back to original data transformations
  • Absence of confidence scores or uncertainty quantification for generated insights
  • Lack of standardized formats for provenance representation across multi-insight narratives

This opacity undermines trust and prevents users from verifying insights independently.

2.3 Inadequate Evaluation Frameworks

Existing evaluation methods for NLG systems are ill-suited for assessing factual correctness:

  • Text similarity metrics (BLEU, ROUGE) reward fluent hallucinations that match reference style
  • No standardized benchmarks for numeric faithfulness in tabular data analysis
  • Lack of automated verification tools for numeric claims
  • Human evaluation protocols that prioritize fluency over accuracy
  • Absence of task-based evaluation measuring decision quality improvement

This evaluation gap allows incorrect systems to appear competent, delaying progress toward truly faithful insight generation.

2.4 Poor Domain Generalization and Adaptation

Current systems struggle with domain-specific requirements:

  • Inability to adapt terminology and narrative style to different audiences (executives vs. analysts)
  • Poor handling of domain-specific constraints and regulatory requirements
  • Limited robustness to varying data quality, missing values, and encoding variations
  • Difficulty incorporating domain knowledge and expert priors
  • One-size-fits-all approaches that ignore domain-specific insight taxonomies

2.5 Research Gap Synthesis

Critical Research Gap: There exists no comprehensive framework that simultaneously addresses faithfulness through numeric verification, provides transparent grounding and provenance, employs rigorous evaluation focused on factual correctness, and adapts to domain-specific requirements—while maintaining the narrative fluency that makes LLMs valuable for insight generation in the first place.

3. Research Objectives

Primary Objective: To develop a comprehensive framework for explainable, faithful, and evaluable insight generation from tabular data using Large Language Models.

  • Develop a Hybrid Grounded Insight Generation Framework that combines deterministic statistical computation with LLM-based narrative generation and explicit grounding mechanisms.
  • Design and Implement a Numeric Auditor for automated verification that parses generated insights, re-computes claimed values, and identifies discrepancies.
  • Create a Comprehensive Benchmark comprising diverse tabular datasets with expert-annotated insights, cell-level grounding, and evaluation toolkits.
  • Develop Novel Evaluation Methods including automatic faithfulness metrics, grounding quality metrics, and human evaluation protocols.
  • Conduct User Studies to assess task completion, trust calibration, comprehension, and perceived utility in professional workflows.

Key Findings from Literature Analysis

Faithfulness Issues

Evidence: Chart-to-Text systems show persistent factuality issues. LLMs hallucinate trends not present in data.

Gap: No systematic post-generation verification mechanisms.

Grounding Limitations

Evidence: ToTTo shows promise with cell highlighting but limited to single sentences.

Gap: No standard format for provenance representation across multi-insight narratives.

Evaluation Weaknesses

Evidence: BLEU/ROUGE reward fluent hallucinations. G-EVAL improves but lacks data verification.

Gap: No automated numeric verification tools or task-based evaluation protocols.

Trust Understudied

Evidence: Few empirical studies on user interaction with AI-generated insights.

Gap: Unknown how grounding affects trust or decision quality.

8. Framework Design Progress

Status: 40% Complete

Proposed Hybrid Architecture (4-Layer System)

Layer 1: Deterministic Statistical Computation

Trend detection, anomaly identification, seasonal decomposition, correlation analysis, aggregations using Pandas, NumPy, SciPy, statsmodels

Layer 2: LLM-Based Narrative Generation

Translate statistical findings to natural language, adapt tone to audience, generate structured grounding with cell references

Layer 3: Numeric Auditor (Verification)

Extract numeric claims, re-compute from source data, compare and flag discrepancies, generate audit reports

Layer 4: Visualization Generation & Alignment

Generate Vega-Lite/Altair specs, highlight referenced cells, attach confidence scores, link narratives to charts

Insight Taxonomy (15 Categories Defined)

Developed comprehensive taxonomy covering: Trend, Seasonal Pattern, YoY Change, Anomaly/Outlier, Top-K/Bottom-K, Correlation, Segment Comparison, Rate of Change, Distribution Characteristics, Threshold Crossing, Composition, Concentration, Seasonally Adjusted Trend, Change Point Detection, and Forecast Deviation.
